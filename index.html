SOLEY Suite

Photovoltaic simulation from semiconductor physics to real-world energy production. Design any solar cell from first principles. Simulate it outdoors, indoors, or in space.

The platform

Four modules, one physics engine

SOLEY Suite uses an approach different from the drift-diffusion model commonly found in PV simulation. It combines a full Transfer Matrix Method optical engine (including light scattering and light trapping models, which is rare for TMM implementations) with an extended detailed balance electrical model. The result is a faster computation that yields accuracy comparable to drift-diffusion solvers for most practical device configurations.

All four modules share this single validated engine. Results are consistent whether you simulate a rooftop installation or a solar panel on the lunar surface.

SOLEY Core

The physics engine behind every module.

  • Full TMM optics for arbitrary thin-film layer stacks, with partial coherence, angular dependence, and polarisation control
  • Debye-Waller scattering and geometric light trapping on top of TMM, a rare combination that handles both smooth and textured interfaces
  • Extended detailed balance electrical model: SRH, Auger, photon recycling, series and shunt resistance, Hecht collection
  • J-V curves, EQE, photoluminescence, electroluminescence, defect analysis, batch parameter sweeps
  • Built-in theoretical hints: long-form explanations of the equations and methods behind every calculation

Fig. 1 -- SOLEY Core. J-V characteristics computed from a user-defined thin-film stack.

I-SOLEY

Outdoor energy production, hour by hour.

  • Full-year hourly simulation using real meteorological data (ERA5, SARAH-2/3, NSRDB)
  • TMM-based angle-resolved optics at every timestep, not simplified absorption coefficients
  • Perez sky diffuse irradiance model for accurate decomposition of direct and diffuse components
  • Martinez-Moreno self-shading model with row-to-row shading, bypass diode logic, and interactive shading diagrams
  • Four tracking modes, three cell temperature models, soiling, ageing, bifacial
  • Financial analysis: NPV, IRR, LCOE, Monte Carlo risk assessment

Fig. 2 -- I-SOLEY. Annual energy production from a full-year hourly simulation.

IN-SOLEY

Indoor photovoltaics. No existing tool does this.

  • Full radiosity solver for artificial lighting environments
  • Anisotropic fixture emission, extended source model, window daylight
  • Coupled TMM optics: the same device physics as outdoor simulation
  • Batch optimisation of cell placement, angle, and height

Fig. 3 -- IN-SOLEY. Indoor light distribution and photovoltaic energy harvesting simulation.

SOLEY-S

Solar panels on the Moon. We simulate that.

  • Transient electro-thermal simulation using JPL DE440s ephemeris
  • Original Koltsov & Jehl Li-Kao thermal balance model: 6 absorption terms, radiative cooling, regolith coupling
  • Five degradation mechanisms, adaptive timestepping
  • Presets for lunar surface, LEO, Mars, deep space

Fig. 4 -- SOLEY-S. Cell temperature and power output during a lunar day-night cycle.

Every module shares the same validated physics engine. A device designed in SOLEY Core can be tested on a rooftop in Barcelona, under an office ceiling lamp, or on the lunar surface. Same optics, same device physics, consistent results across environments.

All utility-scale modules (I-SOLEY, IN-SOLEY, SOLEY-S) produce detailed executive reports and give full access to the raw output data for further analysis.

Who it serves

Built for people who work with photovoltaics

Educators

SOLEY is the only platform that takes students from semiconductor physics all the way to utility-scale production simulation in a single environment. Built-in theoretical hints explain every equation and method used in the calculations. One tool covers device optics, electrical modelling, outdoor production, indoor harvesting, and space PV.

Students

For a bachelor thesis, a master project, or self-study, SOLEY provides a complete PV simulation environment with no steep learning curve. The academic version of SOLEY Core is free and always will be.

Researchers

A unified framework for simulating almost any thin-film photovoltaic technology: perovskite, CIGS, CdTe, GaAs, silicon, tandems, multi-junction devices. Fundamental researchers can test lab-scale digital twins in real-world conditions. Those working further downstream, for example on LCA analysis, get reliable models grounded in device physics rather than empirical approximations.

Industry

SOLEY helps with the development of emerging technologies for which empirical data is scarce, and with emerging PV applications: building-integrated PV, agrivoltaics, indoor PV for IoT. It does not try to replace established empirical models, but it offers physics-based estimation of PV production where no empirical model exists yet. Indoor PV and space PV modelling are virtually uncharted. SOLEY is the first tool to cover them.
